40 Famous Names of Lord Krishna

Explore some of the most cherished names of Shri Krishna and understand the profound meanings behind them.

Govinda (गोविन्द) – The protector of cows and Earth
Gopal (गोपाल) – The caretaker of cows
Keshava (केशव) – The one with beautiful hair
Madhava (माधव) – Husband of the Goddess of Fortune
Murari (मुरारी) – The destroyer of demon Mura
Vasudeva (वासुदेव) – Son of Vasudeva
Hari (हरि) – The one who removes sins
Shyam (श्याम) – The dark-skinned Lord
Jagannatha (जगन्नाथ) – The Lord of the Universe
Parthasarathi (पार्थसारथी) – Charioteer of Arjuna
Damodara (दामोदर) – The Lord tied with a rope by Yashoda
Achyuta (अच्युत) – The infallible one
Radhanath (राधानाथ) – The Lord of Radha
Yadunandan (यदुनन्दन) – Son of the Yadu dynasty
Murlimanohar (मुरलीमनोहर) – The flute-playing Lord who enchants all
Nanda Kumar (नंदकुमार) – Son of Nanda
Giridhari (गिरिधारी) – The lifter of Govardhan Hill
Bihari (बिहारी) – The playful one
Balagopal (बालगोपाल) – The divine child Krishna
Madhusudan (मधुसूदन) – The slayer of the demon Madhu
Rasik (रसिक) – The supreme enjoyer
Mohan (मोहन) – The enchanter
Adhokshaja (अधोक्षज) – Beyond material perception
Vishnu (विष्णु) – The all-pervading Lord
Satyananda (सत्यानंद) – The embodiment of eternal bliss
Gyaneshwar (ज्ञानेश्वर) – The Lord of wisdom
Lila Purushottama (लीला पुरुषोत्तम) – The divine player
Anantavikrama (अनंतविक्रम) – The one with infinite power
Ranchod (रणछोड़) – The one who left the battlefield (for divine play)
Chaturbhuj (चतुर्भुज) – The four-armed Lord
Karunamayi (करुणामयी) – The merciful one
Padmanabha (पद्मनाभ) – The Lord with a lotus-shaped navel
Sarvapalaka (सर्वपालक) – The protector of all
Shrikrishna (श्रीकृष्ण) – The all-attractive one
Venuvadan (वेणुवादन) – The flute player
Pundarikaksha (पुण्डरीकाक्ष) – Lotus-eyed Lord
Janardana (जनार्दन) – The one who liberates humanity
Shri Hari (श्रीहरि) – The supreme remover of obstacles
Rasraj (रसराज) – The king of divine love
MahaYogi (महायोगी) – The greatest meditator
